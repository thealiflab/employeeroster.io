Features

Everything your team needs, nothing they don't

A complete rostering system built for WordPress — no third-party accounts, no monthly per-seat costs.

📅

Shift Scheduling

Create shifts with direct assignment or open slots. View your team in weekly roster or monthly calendar. Copy an existing roster week to save time rebuilding schedules.

⏱

Clock In / Clock Out

Staff clock in from the WordPress dashboard or the frontend portal. Break tracking and shift linking included. One active session per user enforced.

🏖

Leave Management

Employees submit annual or sick leave requests. Managers approve or decline. Optional leave balance tracking with per-job-type entitlements.

📊

Timesheets & Payroll

Weekly summaries per employee with hours, pay rate calculations, optional tax, and daily labour budget tracking against a configurable target.

🔄

Shift Swap Requests

Employees request swaps between each other. Managers review and approve — assignments update automatically on approval.

📆

Staff Availability

Each employee sets their weekly recurring availability. Availability hints appear inside the shift assignment modal so managers avoid conflicts instantly.

✉️

Email Notifications

Automated plain-text emails via wp_mail() for new accounts, shift assignments, timing changes, and leave decisions. No HTML templates to break.

📱

Frontend Employee Portal

Five shortcode-powered pages let staff check shifts, clock in, request leave, and set availability without ever touching WP Admin.

🔒

Role-Based Access

Separate roles for Managers, Supervisors, and Employees. Profile Mode blocks employee WordPress logins entirely while email notifications keep working.

🌐

Public Roster

Embed a read-only roster on any page with the [emp_roster_public] shortcode — no login required. Great for displaying schedules to customers or walk-in staff.

📋

Availability Hints

When assigning a shift, each employee row shows whether they're available, unavailable, or haven't set availability for that day — so managers avoid scheduling conflicts without leaving the modal.

Setup

Up and running in minutes

1

Install & Activate

Upload to /wp-content/plugins/ and activate. 7 database tables and 3 custom roles are created automatically.

2

Configure

Add your departments, locations, and pay settings from Employee Roster > Settings.

3

Add Staff

Create staff accounts with job type, pay rate, and role. Welcome emails with login links are sent automatically when email notifications are enabled.

4

Build the Roster

Create shifts, assign employees, and publish. Staff see their schedules immediately — in WP Admin or the frontend portal.
